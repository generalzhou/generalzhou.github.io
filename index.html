Dev Bootcamp

Path to Dev Bootcamp

I studied biomedical engineering at Washington University in St. Louis. I interned at Baxter and did some really cool things, including designing a novel infusion device for hemophilia patients. However, I realized that the pace of medical device engineering is on the scale of decades, which was way too slow for me.

After working for a few years in professional services, I realized I really missed engineering and puzzle solving. I applied to Dev Bootcamp in 2012, and recently graduated this June.

Jack Zhou

Jack Zhou was not my first name. I was born in Shanghai, and my birth name is Zhou Chun. I moved to the US when I was 2, and my parents decided to give me an American name. My dad really liked basketball, and he loved Magic Johnson. So they thought that my name should be Johnson. Even in my young age, I knew that having a last name as my first name was weird.

The next year I started kindergarten, and on the first day we had to introduce ourselves. Over the last year, I watched a lot of movies where someone named Jack was always doing something badass (and Jack Nicholson was big back then too). I thought this was a pretty cool name, so I told my teacher and the class that I wanted to be called Jack. The teacher was shocked that someone would just decided to change their name in class, but I persisted, and after they called my parents, I started going by the name Jack Zhou.
